Early Beginnings and Mineral Discoveries

South Africa's business history traces back to the 17th century when European settlers arrived, focusing on trade and agriculture. The late 19th century brought significant mineral discoveries, including diamonds and gold, which led to rushes and drew entrepreneurs from around the world. These mineral resources played a crucial role in shaping the nation's economy.

Apartheid and Economic Isolation

The apartheid era from 1948 to 1994 had a profound impact on South African business. Apartheid's racial segregation limited economic opportunities for non-white citizens, and international sanctions were imposed due to these policies. This period saw the country's isolation and the withdrawal of foreign investments.

Reforms and Diverse Landscape

Following the end of apartheid, South Africa underwent political and economic changes. Democratization, the lifting of sanctions, and economic reforms opened doors to foreign investments. Today, South Africa boasts a diverse business landscape encompassing sectors like finance, technology, mining, and agriculture. The country is a key player in Africa, though it faces ongoing challenges such as inequality and unemployment.
